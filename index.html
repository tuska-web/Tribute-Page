Donald J. Trump

Donald Trump, 45th President of the United States (2017–2021)

Donald John Trump (born June 14, 1946) is an American businessman, television personality, and politician who served as the 45th president of the United States from 2017 to 2021.

Born and raised in Queens, New York City, Trump earned an economics degree from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania. He took charge of his family's real estate business in 1971, renaming it *The Trump Organization* and expanding its operations to include skyscrapers, hotels, casinos, and golf courses.

Trump gained international fame as the host of the reality television series *The Apprentice* (2004–2015). In politics, he is known for his "America First" agenda, tax reform, deregulatory policies, and unconventional leadership style.

Key milestones during his presidency include:

  • The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017
  • Appointment of three Supreme Court justices
  • Normalization agreements between Israel and several Arab states (the Abraham Accords)
  • Management of the COVID-19 pandemic response and vaccine rollout
  • Significant immigration policy changes

Despite facing controversies, impeachments, and polarizing opinions, Donald Trump continues to influence U.S. politics as a prominent figure in the Republican Party.
